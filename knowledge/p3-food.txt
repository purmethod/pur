═══ THE SCIENCE OF FASTING ═══
AUTOPHAGY:
During fasting (typically after 16-18 hours), the body activates autophagy — a cellular cleaning process where damaged and dysfunctional cellular components are broken down and recycled. Nobel Prize in Physiology, 2016 (Yoshinori Ohsumi) — awarded specifically for the discovery of autophagy mechanisms.

Autophagy clears:

- Damaged mitochondria (mitophagy)
- Misfolded proteins (linked to neurodegenerative diseases)
- Damaged organelles
- Inflammatory cellular debris

INSULIN AND HORMONES:
Every time you eat, insulin is released. Insulin is an anabolic hormone — it promotes storage. When insulin is low (fasted state), the body switches to fat burning, growth hormone rises, cellular repair activates.

Chronically elevated insulin (from frequent eating, especially carbohydrates) leads to:

- Insulin resistance
- Chronic inflammation
- Suppressed growth hormone
- Suppressed fat burning
- Hormonal dysregulation

TESTOSTERONE:
Fasting raises testosterone through multiple mechanisms:

- Reduced insulin (insulin and testosterone are inversely related)
- Reduced body fat (adipose tissue converts testosterone to estrogen via aromatase)
- Improved sleep quality (when eating window is early)
- Reduced inflammation

═══ THE OMAD / 21-HOUR FASTING PROTOCOL ═══
PAUL PUR PROTOCOL:

- Fasting window: 19:00–13:00 next day = 18 hours minimum
- No calories during fasting window (water, black coffee, electrolytes permitted)

═══ FOOD QUALITY ═══
WHAT TO EAT:

- Animal proteins: beef, lamb, eggs, fish — complete amino acid profiles, bioavailable nutrients, testosterone-supporting saturated fats and cholesterol
- Organ meats: liver especially — most nutrient-dense food known (vitamins A, B12, iron, zinc, copper)
- Vegetables: leafy greens, cruciferous (broccoli, cauliflower — support estrogen clearance)
- Healthy fats: olive oil, butter, beef tallow, avocado — support hormone production

WHAT TO MINIMIZE:

- Seed oils (vegetable oil, canola, sunflower) — high in omega-6, promote inflammation
- Processed foods — designed to override satiety signals
- Sugar — spikes insulin, promotes inflammation, feeds bad gut bacteria
- Alcohol — suppresses testosterone directly (alcohol is metabolized to acetaldehyde which inhibits testosterone synthesis), disrupts sleep architecture

═══ FOOD AS INFORMATION ═══
Every meal sends a hormonal signal. The composition of your meal determines:

- Insulin response (carbohydrates/sugar)
- Testosterone precursors (saturated fats, cholesterol)
- Inflammation level (omega-6 vs omega-3 ratio)
- Gut microbiome composition (fiber, fermented foods)
- Neurotransmitter production (tryptophan → serotonin, tyrosine → dopamine)

The man who understands this does not count calories. He eats foods that send the right signals.

═══ EMOTIONAL EATING ═══
Eating to calm stress is one of the most common and destructive patterns in modern men.

Mechanism: Stress raises cortisol. High carbohydrate/sugar foods temporarily suppress cortisol via insulin response. This creates a learned pattern: stress → eat → feel calmer → repeat.

The body learns to use food as emotional regulation. This is not hunger. This is addiction.

Solution: recognize the pattern. When the urge to eat arrives outside the eating window, ask: am I hungry or am I stressed? The pause creates awareness. Awareness creates choice.
